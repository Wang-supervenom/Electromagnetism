Coulomb's Law

Explore the fundamentals of Coulomb's Law in electromagnetism.

Overview

Coulomb’s Law quantifies the electrostatic force between two point charges. It states that the magnitude of the force F is directly proportional to the product of the charges (q₁ q₂) and inversely proportional to the square of the distance (r²) separating them:

F = k · (q₁ · q₂) / r²

The force acts along the line connecting the two charges; like charges repel, while unlike charges attract. This law underpins the fundamentals of electrostatics, influencing fields such as chemistry, particle physics, and electrical engineering.

Key Points

  • Equation Structure: Resembles Newton's law of gravitation.
  • Foundational Role: Used at both atomic and engineering scales.
  • Directional Force: Acts along the line connecting two charges.

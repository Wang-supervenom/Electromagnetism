Maxwell's Equations

Explore the unified theory of electromagnetism through Maxwell’s Equations.

Overview

Maxwell’s Equations unify electricity, magnetism, and optics. They describe how electric charges and currents produce electric and magnetic fields, and how these fields interact and propagate.

Equations

  1. Gauss’s Law: ∇·E = ρ / ε₀
  2. Gauss’s Law for Magnetism: ∇·B = 0
  3. Faraday’s Law: ∇×E = -∂B/∂t
  4. Ampère–Maxwell Law: ∇×B = μ₀J + μ₀ε₀∂E/∂t

Key Points

  • Unified Framework: Combines electrostatics, magnetism, and dynamics.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Light is predicted to be an EM wave.
  • Applications: Forms the foundation of communication, optics, and modern physics.

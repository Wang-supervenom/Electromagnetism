Maxwell's Equations

Maxwell's Equations describe the fundamental principles of electromagnetism. They consist of four equations:

1. Gauss's Law

∮ E ⋅ dA = Q / ε₀

Describes the relation between electric charge and electric field.

2. Gauss's Law for Magnetism

∮ B ⋅ dA = 0

States that there are no magnetic monopoles; magnetic field lines always form closed loops.

3. Faraday's Law

∮ E ⋅ dl = - dΦB / dt

Describes how a changing magnetic field induces an electric field (electromagnetic induction).

4. Ampère-Maxwell Law

∮ B ⋅ dl = μ₀ (I + ε₀ dΦE / dt)

Describes how electric currents and changing electric fields produce magnetic fields.

These equations form the foundation of electromagnetism and are crucial for understanding light, radio waves, and wireless communication.

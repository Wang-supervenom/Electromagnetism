Electric Field

Explore the fundamentals of electric fields in electromagnetism.

Overview

An electric field E is a vector field generated by electric charges or varying magnetic fields. It represents the force a test charge q would experience at a point in space, defined as:

E = F / q

Electric fields govern how charges influence one another over distance and are central to electrostatic induction, potential, and material behavior.

Key Points

  • Direction & Magnitude: Direction points where a positive test charge would move.
  • Superposition: Fields from multiple sources add vectorially.
  • Applications: Used in capacitors, sensors, high-voltage engineering.

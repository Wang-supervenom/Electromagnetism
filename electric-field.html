Electric Field

The electric field (E) is a vector field that describes the influence that a charge exerts on other charges in its vicinity. It is defined as the force per unit charge:

E = F / q

where:

  • E is the electric field strength (N/C)
  • F is the force exerted on a small test charge (N)
  • q is the test charge (Coulombs, C)

Electric fields are created by electric charges and influence other charges through electrostatic force. They are fundamental in understanding capacitors, electrostatic shielding, and electric potential.

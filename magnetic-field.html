Magnetic Field

Explore the fundamentals of magnetic fields in electromagnetism.

Overview

A magnetic field B is a vector field produced by moving charges (currents) or intrinsic magnetic moments. The force on a charge q moving with velocity v in a magnetic field is:

F = q (v Ã— B)

Magnetic fields are essential to electromotors, transformers, and induction. Their field lines form closed loops; no magnetic monopoles are known to exist.

Key Points

  • Origin: Arises from moving charges or magnetic materials.
  • Direction: Determined by the right-hand rule.
  • Applications: Found in motors, MRI, magnetic levitation, etc.

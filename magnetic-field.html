Magnetic Field

The magnetic field (B) is a vector field that describes the magnetic influence of electric currents and magnetized materials. It is defined through the force experienced by a moving charge:

F = q(v Ã— B)

where:

  • F is the force on the charge (N)
  • q is the charge (C)
  • v is the velocity of the charge (m/s)
  • B is the magnetic field strength (Tesla, T)

Magnetic fields are produced by moving electric charges and currents. They are essential for understanding electromagnetism, generators, electric motors, and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI).
